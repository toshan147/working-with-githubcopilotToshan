## Recommended Action Plan to Restart Career

### Immediate Steps (Month 1–2)
1. **Update/create LinkedIn profile** — Highlight B.Com degree, freelance finance experience, and transferable skills from homemaking (organisation, multitasking, budgeting)
2. **Register on Polish job portals** — Pracuj.pl, NoFluffJobs, JustJoinIT, LinkedIn Jobs
3. **Register at the local Urząd Pracy (Labour Office)** — Access to free training programmes, career counselling, and job placement services for residents
4. **Prepare a CV in both English and Polish** — Use the Europass format widely accepted in the EU

### Short-term Upskilling (Month 1–3)
5. **Take a basic Polish language course** — Even A1/A2 level significantly improves job prospects; many free courses available through Urząd Pracy or local integration centres
6. **Complete Excel/Google Sheets certification** — Google Sheets certification is free; advanced Excel is highly valued
7. **Take a free SAP overview course** — OpenSAP offers free introductory courses
8. **Complete a basic accounting/bookkeeping refresher** — Coursera, Udemy, or Khan Academy

### Medium-term Goals (Month 3–6)
9. **Apply for entry-level roles in SSCs** — Target Accounts Payable, Accounts Receivable, or Finance Operations roles
10. **Consider Polish language B1 certification** — Opens doors to many more roles
11. **Network through expat communities** — Wrocław has active expat groups (InterNations, Facebook groups) where job leads are shared
12. **Explore return-to-work programmes** — Some companies (e.g., UBS, Capgemini) have specific programmes for women returning after career breaks

### Long-term Development (6–12 months)
13. **Consider a postgraduate diploma or certification** — ACCA (Association of Chartered Certified Accountants), or a Polish university's postgraduate programme in finance/HR
14. **Build towards specialisation** — Choose between finance, HR, or administration based on initial work experience and interest
